Phase 1 — Active

KOSL Albu

Egg white lithography

We coat glass slides with egg white mixed with vitamin B2, blast it with a 5W 450nm laser on a $525 CNC, and develop in water. The riboflavin generates singlet oxygen which crosslinks the ovalbumin protein, making exposed areas water-insoluble.

Right now we're in Phase 1: proving this actually works at 100μm under a microscope. Once that's confirmed, we start building transistors.

Roadmap

Albu development phases

The plan, from "does egg white even work" to "we built a CPU."

Phase 1 — Current
Photoresist Validation
Does egg white photoresist actually work at 450nm? Shoot a pattern with the CNC laser, develop in water, check under the microscope. We need to see 100μm features on glass.
$525 setup CNC 3018 + laser
Phase 2
First Transistors
Make actual transistors. ZnO TFTs on glass, Schottky-barrier MOSFETs, laser-doped silicon, Cu2O p-type. Goal: a 10-transistor ring oscillator that actually oscillates.
ZnO TFT Si MOSFET Ring oscillator
Phase 2.5
Automated Lithography Upgrade
Ditch the CNC laser for a DLP projector — way faster, whole-field exposure. Semi-automate the spin coating and development. This becomes the 50MT500 product.
50μm features DLP projection
Phase 3
Full Automated Fab Line
The whole process, automated end-to-end. Precision stages, HEPA box, machine vision alignment. 10 engineering sub-phases to get through. This is the big one.
10μm features ~5,000 transistors
Phase 4
First CPU
Make a CPU. Either a custom 4-bit design (~910 transistors) or a SERV RISC-V core (~4,500 transistors). Depends on how good the yields are from Phase 3.
4-bit or RISC-V Up to 4,500 transistors
Future
DUV Stepper
Schwarzschild reflective objective + surplus KrF excimer laser = 500nm features. That's Pentium-class density. Millions of transistors. This is the long game.
500nm features Millions of transistors
