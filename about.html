Our Mission

Chips shouldn't require a billion-dollar factory

KOSL exists because the ability to fabricate semiconductors shouldn't be locked behind billion-dollar fabs and toxic chemical supply chains. We're building the processes, tools, and knowledge base to make chip-making as accessible as making PCBs.

Every process we develop follows three principles: no toxic chemicals, no cleanroom required, and no million-dollar equipment. We replace hydrofluoric acid with drain cleaner, PMMA photoresist with egg whites, and clean-room spinners with desktop CNC machines.

Our Approach

Grocery store chemistry

Our core innovation is the complete substitution of every hazardous chemical in the semiconductor fabrication process with safe, grocery-store-available alternatives.

Egg white protein (ovalbumin) serves as both photoresist and gate dielectric. Riboflavin — vitamin B2 — acts as a photosensitizer at 450nm. Development happens in plain water. Etching uses potassium hydroxide (drain cleaner). Everything is non-toxic and available off the shelf.

Values

What drives us

Open Source

All designs, process recipes, equipment schematics, and experiment results published under permissive licenses. No patents, no paywalls.

Accessible

No specialty chemicals or restricted materials. No used equipment from decommissioned fabs. Everything from Amazon and the grocery store.

Safe

No HF. No organic solvents. Water-based development. Grocery store etchants. Safe enough for a garage or university teaching lab.
