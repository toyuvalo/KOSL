About

What KOSL is

A lab in Toronto trying to make chips without the billion-dollar fab.

The idea

PCBs used to be hard too

Twenty years ago, making a PCB at home was a pain. Now you can order one from JLCPCB for two bucks. We think chip fabrication is headed the same way, but someone has to figure out the process first.

That's us. We're replacing every toxic, expensive, restricted chemical in semiconductor fab with stuff you can buy at a grocery store. HF becomes drain cleaner. Photoresist becomes egg whites. The developer is water.

How it works

Grocery store chemistry

Egg white protein (ovalbumin) works as both photoresist and gate dielectric. Riboflavin — vitamin B2 — is the photosensitizer. Hit it with a 450nm laser and the protein crosslinks. Wash off the unexposed parts with water. Done.

The ovalbumin also has a dielectric constant of ~5-6, higher than SiO2. So the same egg white that patterns your features also works as your gate insulator. Cost per mL: about a third of a cent.

Principles

The rules

Open Source

Everything goes on GitHub. Process recipes, schematics, experiment results, failures included. No patents, no paywalls, no "contact us for pricing."

Accessible

If you can't buy it on Amazon or at a grocery store, we don't use it. No "well you just need to find a decommissioned fab" hand-waving.

Safe

No hydrofluoric acid. No organic solvents. Nothing that'll kill you if you spill it. You should be able to do this in your garage without worrying.
